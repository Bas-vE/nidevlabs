Syntax
public abstract class BorderNode : Wireable, IPropertyLocalStorage, IParsable, IQueryService, IViewElement, IWireShapingElement, ICompositeWireJoint, IEnsureOwnView
Remarks

A BorderNode is a Wireable that is connects wires in different diagrams. A BorderNode is always a child of a Structure. Hence a BorderNode's Structure property is the strongly typed version of its Owner property. The BorderNode can connect to wires in diagrams related to it's Parent Structure. See the documentation for Structure on its related diagrams.

The BorderNode's Relationship and Multiplicity properties as well as all of its terminal's Role properties should be set so that when the BorderNode is added to a structure's BorderNodes collection the BorderNodeTerminals can be aliased correctly. If terminals are added or removed from a BorderNode after it has been added to a Structure than the caller is responsible for setting their Role and aliasing them to the correct Structure or NestedDiagram.

A BorderNode has a collection of BorderNodeTerminals. The terminals can be accessed through the Terminals property or the BorderNodeTerminals property. The BorderNode's terminal's are used by its Structure and or the Structure's NestedDiagrams to connect wires. For a Structure or NestedDiagram to connect to a wire they must use the BorderNode's terminals. The BorderNode's terminals are added to the Structure's or NestedDiagram's terminal collection as aliases. The exact alias parent of a BorderNode terminal is dependent on the Relationship of the BorderNode and the Role of the BorderNodeTerminal. See Role documentation for more information.

The property Relationship determines the set of diagrams the BorderNode can connect wires. The two types of relationships are SiblingToSibling and AncestorToDescendant. A SiblingToSibling relationship means the BorderNode can connect wires in the NestedDiagrams of the Structure. The AncestorToDescendant relationships means the BorderNode can connect wires between the Structure's Parent Diagram and NestedDiagrams in the Structure.

The property Multiplicity determines the number of diagrams the BorderNode can connect wires. A multiplicity of OneToOne means the BorderNode can only connect a single wire in one diagram with a single wire in another diagram. The multiplicity OneToMany means the diagram can connect a single diagram to multiple other diagrams. The ManyToMany multiplicity is not current supported.

The property Docking determines the orientation of the BorderNode with respect to its parent Structure. A change in the Docking property MUST move the terminals of the BorderNode appropriately so they appear in the correct locations.

BorderNodes serialize ID, Bounds, and BorderNodeTerminals. BorderNodeTerminals serialization is the responsibility of derived classes so that they can govern whether to serialize all terminals or only connected terminals on an individual basis. Serialized properties are exposed via dynamic properties.
